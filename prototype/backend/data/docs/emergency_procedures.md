## Purpose

This document establishes procedures for emergency and high-priority claims requiring immediate action beyond standard FNOL processing. All staff must be familiar with these procedures and able to execute them without delay.

---

## 1. Catastrophic Loss Protocol (CAT Events)

The protocol activates when the National Weather Service issues a Severe Thunderstorm Warning, Tornado Warning, or Flood Warning for Douglas, Sarpy, Lancaster, Washington, Saunders, Cass, or Dodge counties.

**Activation Steps:**

1. Agency principal sends CAT activation via group text and email.
2. All available staff report to the office (or log in remotely) within two hours, or by 7:00 AM for overnight events.
3. Claims inbox and phones are staffed continuously during activation.
4. Critical and High priority claims are processed first.
5. Critical claims are reported to the carrier by phone immediately; all others within four hours.
6. Operations lead maintains a CAT tracking spreadsheet (claim, carrier status, adjuster assignment).
7. Deactivation occurs when volume returns to normal.

**Nebraska Considerations:** Tornado season is April–June. Hail events produce widespread roof/siding damage and high FNOL volumes. Missouri/Platte River flooding may trigger NFIP claims — remind insureds that standard homeowners excludes flood.

## 3. Situations Involving Injuries

1. **Notify the carrier immediately by phone.** Do not wait for portal submission.
2. **Do not discuss liability or fault** with anyone. Avoid "we'll take care of this" or "that should be covered."
3. **Document carefully:** names and contact info of injured parties, EMS response, hospital, nature of injuries.
4. **Advise the insured** to cooperate with the carrier's investigation and contact the adjuster.
5. **Notify the agency principal** by end of business, or immediately for hospitalization, surgery, or fatality.
6. **Do not provide medical referrals or legal advice.**

---

## 4. Active or Ongoing Damage

When damage is currently in progress (water intrusion, fire, wind damage to compromised structure):

1. **Safety first.** If any risk to life, instruct the insured to call 911 and evacuate.
2. **Instruct mitigation.** Shut off water, move valuables from leaks, cover broken windows — only if safe to do so.
3. **Remind the insured that mitigation expenses are typically covered.** Save all receipts for tarps, plywood, emergency plumber, water extraction.
4. **Provide emergency vendor referrals** from the agency's vetted restoration vendor list (shared drive and front desk).
5. **Notify the carrier immediately by phone.** Active damage is Critical priority.

---

## 5. Commercial Vehicle Accidents

- **DOT Reporting:** Accidents involving CMVs over 10,001 lbs GVWR or hazmat vehicles may trigger DOT reporting. Remind the insured (motor carrier) of compliance obligations.
- **FMCSA Requirements:** Post-accident drug/alcohol testing, evidence preservation, FMCSA reporting for qualifying accidents (fatality, medical transport injury, or disabling damage requiring tow).
- **Cargo Concerns:** Document cargo type and estimated value. For hazmat spills, instruct the insured to follow their response plan and contact emergency services — do not approach the scene.
- **Notify the carrier immediately by phone.** Provide driver name, CDL number, vehicle unit number, and all accident details.

---

## 6. Livestock Emergencies

- **Veterinary Care:** Advise the insured to contact their veterinarian immediately for animal welfare and loss documentation.
- **Temporary Fencing:** If livestock escaped due to fence damage, advise the insured to secure animals and arrange temporary fencing. Mitigation expenses are typically covered.
- **Documentation:** Photograph the scene, injured/deceased animals, and cause of loss (downed fence, lightning evidence, predator tracks). Preserve carcasses for potential adjuster inspection if practical.
- **Carrier Notification:** Report to Grinnell Mutual (primary farm/ranch carrier) within four hours via phone or email. Include species, breed, head count, value per head, and cause.

---

## 7. Workers' Compensation Injuries

1. **Employer Reporting:** Confirm the employer has documented the injury and will file a First Report of Injury with the Nebraska Workers' Compensation Court (required within 10 days if the employee misses more than one workday or needs treatment beyond first aid).
2. **Medical Provider:** Under Nebraska law, the employer generally directs initial treatment. Confirm they have a designated provider or refer to the carrier.
3. **Carrier Notification:** Report immediately with employee name, job title, date/time, incident description, and injury nature.
4. **OSHA Reporting:** Remind the employer — fatality within **8 hours**, hospitalization/amputation/eye loss within **24 hours**. Report via 1-800-321-OSHA or osha.gov.
5. **Do not provide legal advice** on employer obligations or employee rights.

## 8. Communication Templates

**What to say:**

"I understand this is a stressful situation. Let me take down the details so I can report this to your carrier right away. Please make sure you and your family are safe first — call 911 if there is any ongoing danger."

"I will report this immediately. The carrier will assign an adjuster who will contact you directly. I will follow up to make sure you have their contact information."

**What NOT to say:**

- Not: "That should be covered." Say: "The adjuster will review your policy and make a coverage determination."
- Not: "The other driver was at fault." Say: "The adjuster will investigate and determine liability."
- Not: "We'll make sure you're taken care of." Say: "We will make sure your claim is reported promptly and accurately."
- Do not discuss limits, deductibles, or exclusions during emergency intake unless the insured asks directly.

---

## 9. Agency Principal Escalation Triggers

Contact the agency principal immediately for:

- Any fatality (insured, third party, employee, or significant livestock loss)
- Injuries requiring hospitalization or surgery
- Estimated losses exceeding $100,000
- Commercial vehicle accidents involving DOT-regulated vehicles
- Government investigation (OSHA, DOT, FMCSA, law enforcement)
- Media involvement or potential media interest
- Insured threatening legal action against the agency
- Potential E&O exposure (lapsed coverage, missed endorsement, coverage gap at time of loss)

# Certificate of Insurance (COI) Procedures

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** Operations Department, Prairie Shield Insurance Group

---

## 1. What Is a Certificate of Insurance?

A Certificate of Insurance (COI) is a standardized document issued by the agency on behalf of a carrier summarizing the insured's coverage — policy types, numbers, effective/expiration dates, and limits. It serves as evidence that the insured carries the described coverage at the time of issuance.

**A certificate is not a policy.** It does not modify, extend, or alter coverage. It conveys no rights to the certificate holder beyond cancellation notification (if applicable). Misstating the scope of a certificate is a leading source of E&O exposure in commercial lines.

---

## 2. When COIs Are Needed

- **Lease Requirements:** Landlords and property managers require tenants to show CGL coverage, often with additional insured status.
- **Contracts and Subcontracts:** General contractors require certificates evidencing GL, auto, workers' comp, and umbrella coverage.
- **Vendor Requirements:** Businesses engaging service vendors (cleaning, landscaping, IT, catering) require proof of adequate coverage.
- **Special Events:** Venues, municipalities, and permitting agencies may require a COI and additional insured endorsement.
- **Loan and Financing:** Lenders require evidence of property insurance showing them as loss payee or mortgagee.

---

## 3. ACORD Certificate Forms

- **ACORD 25 — Certificate of Liability Insurance:** Summarizes GL, auto, umbrella, and workers' comp. The most commonly used form for landlords, contractors, and vendor requests.
- **ACORD 27 — Evidence of Property Insurance:** Property coverage on a specific building/location. Used for lenders, mortgagees, and loss payees. Shows location, coverage amount, deductible, and loss payee.
- **ACORD 28 — Evidence of Commercial Property Insurance:** Detailed commercial property form showing building/BPP limits, business income, causes of loss form, coinsurance, and deductible.

**Selection rule:** Liability coverage evidence = ACORD 25. Property coverage evidence = ACORD 27 (residential/simple) or ACORD 28 (detailed commercial). Some requests require both.

---

## 4. Additional Insured Requirements

**Endorsement vs. Certificate Language:**
- Additional insured status is conferred only by a policy endorsement, not by certificate language. The certificate merely reports that the endorsement exists.
- The agency must confirm the endorsement with the carrier before issuing any certificate showing additional insured status.
- Common endorsement forms: CG 20 10 (Owners, Lessees, or Contractors) and CG 20 37 (Completed Operations).

**Mistakes to avoid:**
- Listing additional insured on the certificate without a confirmed endorsement — creates direct E&O exposure.
- Assuming a blanket additional insured endorsement covers all requests without a written contract between the parties.
- Adding additional insured language to the "Description of Operations" field as a substitute for an endorsement.

---

## 5. Waiver of Subrogation Requests

A waiver of subrogation prevents the carrier from pursuing recovery against the requesting party after paying a claim.

- Must be added by endorsement — request from the carrier before indicating on the certificate.
- Many carriers charge additional premium. Inform the insured before requesting.
- Blanket waiver endorsements may require a written contract between the parties. Verify it exists.

---

## 6. Processing Timeline

- **Standard COI (no endorsement changes):** Same-day. Issue within four business hours.
- **COI requiring endorsement changes:** 24–48 hours depending on carrier response. Inform the requesting party.
- **Rush requests:** Contact the carrier by phone to expedite. Many carriers issue endorsements same-day.

**Workflow:**
1. Receive the COI request from the insured or certificate holder.
2. Determine whether endorsement changes are needed.
3. If none needed, issue from the AMS and deliver to the insured and certificate holder.
4. If endorsement needed, submit to carrier, wait for confirmation, update the AMS, then issue.
5. Save a copy in the client's AMS file.

---

## 7. Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Promising coverage via the certificate.** Never tell a holder the certificate "protects" or "covers" them.
2. **Unauthorized additional insureds.** Never add without carrier-confirmed endorsement.
3. **Failing to track expirations.** Certificates expire with the policy. Issue renewal certificates proactively.
4. **Altering ACORD forms.** Do not modify pre-printed form language.
5. **Issuing for cancelled/expired policies.** Always verify active status in the AMS first.
6. **Ignoring carrier guidelines** on what can appear in the "Description of Operations" field.

## 9. Record Keeping and Tracking

- **AMS Documentation:** Save every issued certificate with date, certificate holder, form type, and endorsement changes.
- **Expiration Tracking:** Configure AMS reminders 30 days before policy expiration for all clients with active certificates.
- **Cancellation Notifications:** If a policy is cancelled or non-renewed, identify all active certificate holders and notify per cancellation provisions (typically 30 days; 10 days for non-payment).
- **Audit Trail:** Log all endorsement requests submitted in connection with COI issuance.
- **Annual Review:** At each renewal, review certificate history and proactively issue renewal certificates to recurring holders.

# Octank Insurance - First Notice of Loss (FNOL) Guide

## What is FNOL?
FNOL (First Notice of Loss) is the initial report you make to Octank Insurance after an accident or incident. Filing a timely and accurate FNOL is crucial for a smooth claims process.

## When to File an FNOL
File an FNOL as soon as possible after:
- Any vehicle accident (your fault or not)
- Vehicle theft or attempted theft
- Vandalism or malicious damage
- Weather-related damage (hail, flood, fallen tree)
- Animal collision (deer, etc.)
- Windshield or glass damage
- Hit-and-run (even if damage seems minor)

**Important**: You should file an FNOL within **72 hours** of the incident. Delays may affect claim processing.

## What to Collect at the Scene

### Immediately After an Accident
1. **Ensure Safety First**
   - Move to a safe location if possible
   - Turn on hazard lights
   - Call 911 if anyone is injured or there's significant damage
   - Do NOT admit fault at the scene

2. **Document the Scene**
   - Take photos of all vehicles involved (all angles)
   - Photograph the overall scene, road conditions, traffic signs
   - Note weather conditions
   - Photograph any visible injuries
   - Take photos of license plates

3. **Exchange Information**
   - Other driver's name, phone, and address
   - Other driver's insurance company and policy number
   - Other driver's license number
   - Vehicle make, model, year, and license plate
   - Names and contact info of passengers

4. **Get Witnesses**
   - Names and phone numbers of any witnesses
   - Ask if they'd be willing to provide a statement

5. **File a Police Report**
   - Always call police for accidents with injuries
   - Recommended for any accident with significant damage
   - Get the police report number — you'll need this for your claim
   - Request a copy of the report

## Information Needed for FNOL Filing

When you contact Octank to file your FNOL, have the following ready:

### Required Information
- **Your policy number and member ID**
- **Date and time of the incident**
- **Location** (street address or intersection)
- **Description of what happened** (be factual and detailed)
- **Other parties involved** (if applicable)
- **Police report number** (if filed)
- **Whether anyone was injured** (critical for routing)

### Additional Helpful Information
- Photos from the scene
- Witness contact information
- Tow company information (if vehicle was towed)
- Repair shop preference (if you have one)
- Rental car needs

## What Happens After Filing

### Timeline
| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Immediately | FNOL recorded, claim number assigned |
| Within 24 hours | Claims adjuster assigned and contacts you |
| 3-5 business days | Vehicle inspection scheduled and completed |
| 7-10 business days | Damage assessment and estimate prepared |
| 15-30 business days | Claim resolved (approval/denial/settlement) |

### What to Expect
1. **Confirmation**: You'll receive a claim number and confirmation immediately
2. **Adjuster Contact**: Your assigned adjuster will call within 24 hours
3. **Inspection**: The adjuster will arrange a vehicle inspection
4. **Estimate**: You'll receive a damage estimate in writing
5. **Resolution**: Your claim will be approved, denied, or settled

## Special Circumstances

### Injury Accidents
If anyone is injured in the accident:
- An FNOL will be filed, but the claim will be **immediately escalated** to our specialized injury claims team
- A senior adjuster will be assigned within 4 hours
- Medical bill coordination will begin immediately
- You may be contacted by our injury advocate

### Fatality Incidents
In the tragic event of a fatality:
- The claim is **immediately escalated** to our critical claims team
- A dedicated claims manager will be assigned
- Legal support resources will be provided
- Compassionate support services are available 24/7

### Hit-and-Run
If you're the victim of a hit-and-run:
- File a police report immediately
- Your Uninsured Motorist coverage may apply
- Document everything you can remember about the other vehicle
- Check for nearby security cameras that may have captured the incident

### Vehicle Theft
If your vehicle is stolen:
- File a police report immediately (required)
- Provide the police report number when filing your FNOL
- There is a 30-day waiting period before a theft claim is settled
- If the vehicle is recovered during this period, damage will be assessed

## Tips for a Smooth Claims Process
- Be honest and accurate in your description — inconsistencies can delay processing
- Keep all receipts related to the incident (towing, rental car, medical)
- Don't authorize repairs until you've spoken with your adjuster
- Respond promptly to your adjuster's requests for information
- Keep a record of all communications related to your claim

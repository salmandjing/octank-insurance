**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** Operations Department, Prairie Shield Insurance Group

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This manual establishes the standard procedures for First Notice of Loss (FNOL) intake at Prairie Shield Insurance Group. All agency staff — licensed producers, customer service representatives (CSRs), and administrative personnel — must follow these procedures when a policyholder reports a loss.

Proper FNOL handling directly affects claim outcomes, carrier relationships, client retention, and the agency's exposure to errors and omissions (E&O) liability.

---

## 2. Receiving a Claim Report

Claims may be received through any of the following channels. The same information standards apply regardless of intake method.

- **Phone:** Answer within three rings during business hours (8:00 AM–5:00 PM CST). Use the FNOL intake form in the agency management system (AMS) to capture details in real time.
- **Email:** Monitor claims@prairieshieldins.com continuously. Acknowledge receipt within one hour. Transfer details into the AMS before end of business.
- **Walk-In:** Escort the insured to a private meeting area. Use the printed FNOL intake checklist.
- **Online Form:** The website form feeds into the AMS queue. A CSR must review and follow up within two hours during business hours. After-hours submissions must be processed by 9:00 AM the next business day.

---

## 3. Required Information Checklist

Obtain the following for every FNOL. Mark unavailable fields as "pending" — never delay carrier submission solely because a non-critical field is incomplete.

1. **Insured Name** — Full legal name as shown on the declarations page.
2. **Policy Number** — Verify active status, effective date, and expiration date in the AMS.
3. **Date and Time of Loss** — As specific as possible; note a range if the insured is unsure.
4. **Location of Loss** — Full address, city, county, state. For auto losses, include intersection or mile marker.
5. **Description of Loss** — Record the insured's own words. Do not paraphrase.
6. **Injuries (Yes/No)** — If yes, document number of injured parties, names, severity, and EMS response. Flag for priority escalation.
7. **Police or Fire Report** — Report number, department, and responding officer if filed.
8. **Other Parties Involved** — Names, contact info, carrier, policy number, vehicle details.
9. **Photos and Documentation** — Request photos of damage, the scene, and vehicles. Upload to the AMS claim file.
10. **Urgency and Mitigation** — Is damage ongoing? Document any emergency mitigation already performed.

---

## 4. Priority Classification

Assign a priority level immediately upon intake.

- **Critical:** Ongoing damage (fire, uncontrolled water), injuries requiring emergency care, fatalities, imminent danger. **Action:** Notify carrier immediately by phone. Notify agency principal within 15 minutes.
- **High:** Minor injuries, commercial vehicle accidents, workers' compensation injuries, livestock losses. **Action:** Notify carrier within two hours. Notify agency principal by end of business.
- **Elevated:** Large losses over $25,000, multi-vehicle accidents without injuries, time-sensitive needs (hotel, rental, business interruption). **Action:** Notify carrier within four hours.
- **Normal:** Standard minor-damage claims, non-urgent property claims, post-event theft reports. **Action:** Notify carrier within 24 hours.

---

## 5. Carrier Notification Requirements

Most carriers require FNOL submission within 24–48 hours. Refer to the Carrier FNOL Submission Requirements document for specifics.

- **Auto-Owners Insurance:** 24-hour turnaround. Agency portal.
- **Erie Insurance:** 12-hour response preferred. Portal and email.
- **EMC Insurance (EMCASCO):** 24-hour turnaround. Portal required for commercial lines.
- **Grinnell Mutual:** 24-hour turnaround. Phone and email. Supplemental forms for farm/ranch.
- **Westfield Insurance:** 24-hour turnaround. Portal preferred.

Always confirm carrier receipt and obtain a claim number.

---

## 6. Nebraska-Specific Requirements

- **Fault State:** Nebraska is a fault-based (tort) state. Modified comparative negligence bars recovery if the claimant is 50% or more at fault (Neb. Rev. Stat. 25-21,185.09).
- **Prompt Reporting:** Neb. Rev. Stat. sections 44-1536 through 44-1544 (Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act) require prompt investigation and processing. The agency must avoid contributing to delays.
- **Hail and Wind:** The Omaha metro is in a high-frequency severe weather corridor. During hail season (April–September), anticipate FNOL surges. The agency activates its Catastrophic Loss Protocol when the NWS issues warnings for Douglas, Sarpy, Lancaster, or surrounding counties.

---

## 7. Documentation Standards

- Record all FNOL data in the AMS within one hour of intake.
- Name photo attachments: `[PolicyNumber]_[DateOfLoss]_[Description]` (e.g., `HO-4421887_20260301_RoofDamage_01.jpg`).
- Save all email correspondence to the claim activity log.
- Note the staff member who took the report and the date/time of intake.

---

## 8. Follow-Up Procedures

- **Missing Information:** Contact the insured within 24 hours. Send a follow-up email or letter if no response within 48 hours. Document all attempts.
- **Carrier Acknowledgment:** Verify the carrier has assigned a claim number within the expected timeframe. Log it in the AMS.
- **Client Updates:** Provide the insured with the claim number, adjuster name, and adjuster phone number. Follow up weekly until the claim is resolved.

---

## 9. Escalation Protocol

Escalate to the agency principal immediately for:

- Bodily injury requiring hospitalization or resulting in fatality
- Commercial vehicle accidents involving DOT-regulated vehicles
- Insured dissatisfaction with agency handling
- Potential E&O exposure (lapsed policy, missed endorsement, coverage gap)
- High-value losses exceeding $50,000
- Claims attracting media attention or government investigation

Do not attempt to resolve E&O situations independently. Notify the agency principal, document the facts, and do not admit fault or make promises.

# Nebraska Claims Handling Regulations — Quick Reference

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** Compliance Department, Prairie Shield Insurance Group

---

## 1. Nebraska Department of Insurance

The Nebraska Department of Insurance (NDOI) regulates carrier conduct, licensing, rate filings, and consumer complaints. Policyholders may file complaints directly with the NDOI at any time.

- **Address:** 1135 M Street, Suite 300, Lincoln, NE 68508
- **Consumer Hotline:** 1-877-564-7323
- **Website:** https://doi.nebraska.gov

## 2. Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act

Nebraska's Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act (**Neb. Rev. Stat. sections 44-1536 through 44-1544**) defines carrier conduct standards for claims handling. While obligations fall primarily on carriers, agents support compliance through prompt and accurate reporting.

The Act prohibits:

- **Misrepresentation:** Knowingly misrepresenting policy provisions to an insured or claimant.
- **Failure to acknowledge:** Not acting reasonably promptly upon claims communications.
- **Inadequate standards:** Not implementing reasonable standards for prompt investigation.
- **Failure to affirm or deny:** Not making coverage determinations within a reasonable time after proof of loss.
- **Compelling litigation:** Not attempting good-faith settlement where liability is reasonably clear.
- **Low-ball offers:** Settling for less than a reasonable person would believe the claimant is entitled to.
- **Delaying payment:** Withholding settlement on one coverage portion to influence another.

---

## 3. Prompt Investigation Requirements

Carriers must investigate all claims with reasonable diligence. Agency staff should:

- Submit FNOL to the carrier as quickly as possible — every day of agency delay reduces the carrier's investigation window.
- Respond to carrier information requests within 24 hours.
- If the insured reports difficulty reaching the adjuster, document the complaint and escalate to the agency principal.

---

## 4. Written Notification Requirements

Carriers must provide written notification at key stages:

- **Acknowledgment:** Within **15 business days** of claim receipt, including claim number and adjuster contact.
- **Coverage determination:** In writing, citing specific policy provisions if coverage is denied or limited.
- **Settlement offer:** In writing with an explanation of the calculation methodology.
- **Denial:** In writing with specific reasons, policy references, and notice of the insured's right to appeal.

If the insured reports missing carrier communications, document their report and follow up with the carrier immediately.

---

## 5. Fair Settlement Practices

- Settlements must reflect the actual loss value supported by documentation and policy provisions.
- Carriers may not require suit as a precondition to fair settlement.
- Carriers may not require releases extending beyond the scope of the settled claim.
- Insureds who disagree with settlement amounts must be given a valuation explanation and allowed to submit additional documentation.

## 7. Auto Insurance — Nebraska Rules

- **Fault/Tort State:** The at-fault driver's insurance pays for damages.
- **Modified Comparative Negligence:** Recovery is reduced by the claimant's fault percentage and barred entirely at **50% or more** fault (Neb. Rev. Stat. 25-21,185.09).
- **Minimum Coverage:** 25/50/25 liability limits. UM/UIM required unless rejected in writing.
- **Accident Reporting:** Accidents involving injury, death, or property damage over $1,000 must be reported to the Nebraska DMV.

Agency staff must never discuss fault determination with the insured or third parties. Leave liability analysis to the adjuster.

## 8. Homeowners — Nebraska Considerations

- **Right to Choose Contractor:** Insureds may select their own repair contractor. Carriers may recommend but cannot require specific vendors.
- **Anti-Concurrent Causation:** Many policies exclude coverage when covered and excluded perils contribute concurrently (e.g., wind and flood). This is frequently litigated in Nebraska storm damage cases. Do not interpret this clause — refer questions to the adjuster or agency principal.
- **Wind/Hail Deductibles:** Separate wind/hail deductibles of 1–2% of dwelling coverage are common. A $300,000 home with a 2% deductible has a $6,000 wind/hail deductible.

---

## 9. Workers' Compensation — Nebraska Requirements

- **Workers' Compensation Court:** Exclusive jurisdiction over workplace injury disputes in Nebraska.
- **First Report of Injury:** Employers must file with the Nebraska Workers' Compensation Court within **10 days** if the injury causes more than one missed workday or requires treatment beyond first aid.
- **OSHA Requirements:** Fatalities within 8 hours; hospitalizations, amputations, or eye loss within 24 hours. These are separate from state WC requirements.
- **Medical Provider:** The employer generally selects the treating physician. Coordinate with the carrier on provider selection.

## 10. Agent Responsibilities and E&O Exposure

Common E&O risks in claims handling:

- **Delayed reporting** — resulting in late-notice coverage denial.
- **Advising on coverage** — never say a loss "is covered" or "is not covered." Say: "The adjuster will review your policy and make a coverage determination."
- **Poor documentation** — failing to record communications and follow-up activities.
- **Liability statements** — discussing fault with insureds, third parties, or attorneys.
- **Failure to escalate** — not elevating complex or high-value claims to the agency principal.

**The safest practice:** Document everything. Report promptly. Do not offer coverage opinions. Escalate when in doubt.

# Carrier FNOL Submission Requirements

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** Operations Department, Prairie Shield Insurance Group

---

## Overview

This document details FNOL submission requirements for each carrier contracted with Prairie Shield Insurance Group. When in doubt, submit by phone and confirm via portal. A timely report through any channel is always preferable to a late report through the ideal channel.

---

## Auto-Owners Insurance

**Submission Method:** Agency portal (preferred). Phone for Critical/High priority.
**Portal:** https://www.auto-owners.com/agent-portal
**Claims Phone:** 1-888-252-4626 (24/7) | **Fax:** 1-517-323-8717

**Format:** ACORD form. Portal auto-populates from policy data.

**Required Fields:**
- Insured name and policy number
- Date, time, and location of loss
- Description of loss (minimum 50-word narrative for property)
- Type of loss (fire, theft, wind/hail, collision, liability, etc.)
- Injuries — yes/no; if yes, number of injured and severity
- Police/fire report number
- Other parties — names, contact info, insurance info
- Preliminary damage estimate

**Response Time:** 24 hours. Adjuster assigned automatically upon portal submission.

**Notes:** Include VIN and vehicle details for auto claims. Include year built, square footage, and construction type for property. Photos strongly encouraged at initial submission.

## Erie Insurance

**Submission Method:** Portal (preferred) and email. Phone for after-hours/Critical.
**Portal:** https://www.erieinsurance.com/agents
**Email:** claims@erieinsurance.com
**Claims Phone:** 1-800-367-3743 (24/7)

**Format:** Erie's proprietary digital format via portal. Email submissions use ACORD form PDF.

**Required Fields:**
- Insured name, policy number, contact phone
- Date, time, and location of loss
- Detailed description and type of loss
- Injuries — names, nature, EMS response details
- Emergency services information (fire, police, EMS)
- Photos — required at initial submission for property and auto physical damage
- Insured's preferred contact method (phone or email)

**Response Time:** 12 hours. Adjuster or vendor may be dispatched within 24 hours for Omaha-area property claims.

**Notes:** Water damage claims require source identification (supply line, sewer backup, weather, appliance). Erie's portal supports real-time status tracking.

## EMC Insurance (EMCASCO)

**Submission Method:** Portal (required for commercial). Phone for personal lines/after-hours Critical.
**Portal:** https://www.emcins.com/agent-portal
**Claims Phone:** 1-800-362-2227 (24/7) | **Fax:** 1-515-345-2898

**Format:** ACORD form. Commercial claims require supplemental details.

**Required Fields:**
- Insured name, policy number, business entity name (commercial)
- Date, time, and location of loss
- Description including specific business operation affected
- Estimated damage amount — required on all commercial property claims
- Witness information — names, contact details, statements
- Type of loss with commercial subcategory (premises liability, products, business auto, equipment breakdown)
- Business interruption impact — can operations continue? Estimated downtime?

**Response Time:** 24 hours. Business interruption claims may receive expedited handling.

**Notes:** EMC specializes in commercial lines — provide maximum business impact detail. Workers' comp claims require employee job title, tenure, and activity at time of injury. Equipment breakdown claims require manufacturer, model, age, and maintenance history.

## Grinnell Mutual

**Submission Method:** Phone and email accepted. Portal available but not required.
**Portal:** https://www.grinnellmutual.com/agents
**Email:** claims@grinnellmutual.com
**Claims Phone:** 1-800-362-2041 (24/7) | **Fax:** 1-641-269-8585

**Format:** Grinnell custom intake form or ACORD form.

**Required Fields:**
- Insured name and policy number
- Date, time, and location of loss
- Description of loss
- **Type of loss** — must use Grinnell's classification: wind, hail, fire, lightning, theft, vandalism, collision, liability, animal-related, equipment failure, or other
- Injuries — yes/no with details
- Police/fire report number
- Farm/ranch policies: type of structure affected (dwelling, barn, outbuilding, grain bin, livestock shelter, machinery shed, fence)
- Livestock claims: species, breed, number of head, estimated value per head, cause of loss, veterinary records

**Response Time:** 24 hours. Peak season (planting, harvest, severe weather) may extend slightly.

**Notes:** Grinnell specializes in farm/ranch risks. For livestock losses, the insured should contact their veterinarian immediately and preserve deceased animals for potential adjuster inspection. Grain/crop claims require commodity type, quantity, and market value.

## Westfield Insurance

**Submission Method:** Portal (preferred). Phone for after-hours/Critical.
**Portal:** https://www.westfieldinsurance.com/agent-center
**Claims Phone:** 1-800-243-0210 (24/7) | **Fax:** 1-330-887-0605

**Format:** ACORD form via portal. Policy info pre-fills automatically.

**Required Fields:**
- Insured name, policy number, contact information
- Date, time, and location of loss
- Description and type of loss
- Injuries — yes/no with details
- Police/fire report number
- Other parties — names, contact, vehicle, and insurance info
- Prior damage history (pre-existing damage relevant to the claim)

**Response Time:** 24 hours. Instant claim number upon portal submission.

**Notes:** Westfield partners with preferred repair shops for auto claims. Liability claims require a detailed event timeline and any insured-claimant correspondence. Notify Westfield of claims involving independent contractors working on the insured's behalf.

## General Reminders

1. Always confirm carrier receipt and obtain a claim number.
2. Follow up by phone if the carrier does not acknowledge within the expected timeframe.
3. Log claim number, adjuster name, and contact info in the AMS immediately.
4. Provide the insured with claim number and adjuster information as soon as available.
5. Refer to the FNOL Procedures Manual for priority classification and escalation protocols.

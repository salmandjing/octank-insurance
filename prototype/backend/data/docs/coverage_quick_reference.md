# Coverage Types Quick Reference Guide

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Last Revised:** March 1, 2026
**Prepared by:** Operations Department, Prairie Shield Insurance Group

---

## Purpose

This guide provides a concise overview of the major coverage types written by Prairie Shield Insurance Group. It is a reference for agency staff during claims intake, client inquiries, and policy reviews. It does not replace policy contract language — always refer to the declarations page and policy forms for definitive coverage information.

**Do not use this guide to make coverage determinations.** Coverage determinations are made by the carrier's claims department.

---

## 1. Personal Auto

Nebraska requires minimum liability limits of 25/50/25.

- **Bodily Injury Liability (BI):** Pays for injuries the insured causes to others. Per-person/per-accident limits (e.g., 100/300).
- **Property Damage Liability (PD):** Pays for damage the insured causes to another party's property.
- **Collision:** Damage to the insured's vehicle from collision, regardless of fault. Subject to deductible.
- **Comprehensive:** Non-collision losses: theft, vandalism, fire, hail, wind, flood, animal strikes, falling objects, glass. Subject to deductible.
- **Uninsured Motorist (UM):** Covers insured when at-fault driver has no insurance. Required in Nebraska unless rejected in writing.
- **Underinsured Motorist (UIM):** Covers insured when at-fault driver's limits are insufficient. Required unless rejected in writing.
- **Medical Payments (MedPay):** Medical expenses for insured and passengers regardless of fault. No deductible. Limits $1,000–$10,000.
- **Rental Reimbursement:** Rental vehicle during covered repair. Daily/maximum limits (e.g., $50/day, $1,500 max).
- **Roadside Assistance / Towing:** Towing and basic services (jump, flat tire, lockout).

## 2. Homeowners

Prairie Shield writes primarily HO-3 (Special Form) and HO-5 (Comprehensive Form).

- **Coverage A — Dwelling:** Structure and attached structures. Open-peril on both HO-3 and HO-5.
- **Coverage B — Other Structures:** Detached garage, shed, fence. Typically 10% of Coverage A.
- **Coverage C — Personal Property:** Insured's belongings. HO-3: named-peril only. HO-5: open-peril. Sub-limits on jewelry, firearms, silverware, electronics.
- **Coverage D — Loss of Use:** Additional living expenses when home is uninhabitable. Typically 20% of Coverage A.
- **Coverage E — Personal Liability:** BI/PD the insured causes to others (non-auto). Includes defense costs. $100,000–$500,000.
- **Coverage F — Medical Payments to Others:** Medical expenses for persons injured on premises, regardless of fault. $1,000–$5,000 per person.

**Common Exclusions:** Flood (requires separate NFIP or private policy), earthquake, maintenance/wear, sewer/drain backup (endorsement available — recommended for Omaha-area homes).

**Wind/Hail Deductibles:** Separate 1–2% of Coverage A is common in Nebraska. A $300,000 dwelling with 2% = $6,000 wind/hail deductible.

## 3. Commercial Auto

- **Combined Single Limit (CSL):** Single BI/PD limit per occurrence. Common: $500,000–$1,000,000.
- **Split Limits:** Separate BI per person, BI per accident, and PD limits.
- **Hired and Non-Owned Auto:** Liability for rented vehicles (hired) or employee personal vehicles (non-owned) used for business.
- **MCS-90 Endorsement:** Required for FMCSA-regulated motor carriers. Guarantees minimum $750,000–$1,000,000 liability to the public for vehicles over 10,001 lbs GVWR in interstate commerce.

---

## 4. Commercial Property / BOP

- **Building Coverage:** Structure including permanent fixtures and installed equipment. Replacement cost or ACV.
- **Business Personal Property (BPP):** Contents — inventory, furniture, equipment, supplies. Separate limit.
- **Business Income:** Lost net income and continuing expenses during covered shutdown. Includes extended period of indemnity.
- **Equipment Breakdown:** Sudden/accidental breakdown of mechanical, electrical, and pressure equipment (boilers, HVAC, electrical panels, computers). Usually added by endorsement.

## 5. Farm and Ranch

Specialty of Grinnell Mutual and other contracted carriers.

- **Farm Dwelling:** Farmhouse and attached structures, similar to homeowners Coverage A.
- **Farm Structures:** Barns, grain bins, silos, machine sheds, livestock shelters. Each scheduled separately.
- **Farm Personal Property:** Harvested crops, hay, feed, seed, fertilizer, supplies. Scheduled or blanket.
- **Livestock:** Death/destruction from covered perils (lightning, fire, wind, vehicle collision). Per-head, per-species, or blanket. Perils and exclusions vary by carrier.
- **Machinery and Equipment:** Tractors, combines, planters, tillage equipment. Scheduled or blanket. Covers collision, fire, theft, vandalism, overturn.
- **Farm Liability:** BI/PD from farming operations, farm visitors, farm stands, and animal incidents. Separate from dwelling liability.

## 6. Workers' Compensation

- **Coverage A — Workers' Compensation:** Statutory benefits per the Nebraska Workers' Compensation Act. Medical, disability, rehabilitation, death benefits. No dollar limit.
- **Coverage B — Employers' Liability:** Employer liability outside the WC statute (third-party-over, dual-capacity). Standard: $100,000/$500,000/$100,000.
- **Exclusions:** Intentional employer acts, independent contractors, regulatory penalties.
- **Return-to-Work:** Many carriers offer programs for modified/full duty transition.

---

## 7. Commercial General Liability (CGL)

- **Premises and Operations:** BI/PD from business premises or ongoing operations.
- **Products and Completed Operations:** BI/PD from products sold or work completed.
- **Personal and Advertising Injury:** Libel, slander, defamation, false arrest, privacy invasion, advertising copyright infringement.
- **Standard Limits:** $1M per occurrence / $2M general aggregate / $2M products-completed operations / $1M personal and advertising injury.

---

## 8. Umbrella / Excess Liability

- **Following Form:** Additional limits above underlying policies (auto, CGL, employers' liability).
- **Self-Insured Retention (SIR):** Typically $10,000 for claims the umbrella covers but no underlying policy does.
- **Drop-Down Coverage:** Umbrella may provide primary coverage when underlying aggregates are exhausted (subject to SIR).
- **Common Limits:** $1,000,000–$5,000,000 for most Prairie Shield commercial clients.
